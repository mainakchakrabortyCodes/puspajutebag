Types of Rugs - A Guide for HomeOwners

Types of Rugs

Be it a small house or a large villa, or even a commercial space, every property deserves to be appealing and lovely. Home decorative accessories are the piece of interior design that beautifies a house. Rugs appear to be the most important and expensive home decor products among the variety of home accessories available in the room decoration segment.

If you have a sofa, a rug is a must-have item. Since without one, the space will look incomplete. In terms of rugs or carpets, there are infinite feasible styles. The weaving, design, and fabrics frequently set them apart. They differ in terms of the materials they are made of.

In this blog post, we will try to educate our visitors about different types of rugs (based on material) and help them to choose the best one.

Wool Rugs

Are you looking to achieve a royal appearance that is also aesthetically pleasing? A wool rug is all you need. One can find a limited variety of woollen rugs in the market. Oriental rugs made of wool and silk have a traditional feel, whereas shaggy wool rugs have a more modern appearance. Wool Persian rugs instantly transform a space into a classic one. These rugs are handmade & the process can take months or even longer. Although pricey, they are worthwhile to purchase as a family heirloom.

Wool rugs are resilient to heavy foot traffic and cosy for the feet. Since wool comes from sheep, it contains oil, which shields it from stains. Due to fibre density, thicker carpets can be made.

But they have two backdrops. They need extensive and proper care because of their thickness and how easily they can pill or shed. Moreover, wool rugs are a more expensive alternative for your house, and many people with low budgets can't still afford them.

Jute and Sisal Rugs

Jute and sisal rugs and carpets are the most well-known variants in the same segment. These two rugs are made of natural fibers and can withstand high traffic. For people allergic to wool rugs (due to animal parts) and have not much budget to afford one, jute rugs are said to be a great substitute. Rug manufacturers now offer jute variants in several hues and patterns, such as spiral and geometrical ones. They appear fashionable and eco-friendly too. It is feasible to clean these rugs at home. So, a budget-friendly yet the finest piece of home decor.

Silk and Viscose Rugs

Silk and viscose rugs are uncommon in this segment. Fibers like silk and viscose come from nature. Bamboo is the source of viscose, which is why it is also known as bamboo silk. We are all aware that silkworms are used to produce silk fiber. Silk rugs are thin and glossy. Being fragile, experts advise using them as hanging objects. Like wool, they can also be a reason for allergy. Silk rugs are also not pocket-friendly.

Synthetic Fiber Rugs

Synthetic fiber rugs or synthetic rugs are currently buzzing in the market. One can find varieties of synthetic fiber exist in shops. Synthetic rugs are lightweight, affordable & ready-to-get one. They are inexpensive machine-made rugs that are a good option for houses. These rugs come in a variety of sizes, designs, and colors. They are the best choice for homeowners on a tight budget. Nylon, olefin, and polyester are some of the most sought-after materials used to make synthetic rugs.

But because of environmental pollution, they might not suit your home. As humans need a more sustainable environment to lead our life shortly, we advise people not to use synthetic rugs that are completely harmful to nature as well as the human body.
